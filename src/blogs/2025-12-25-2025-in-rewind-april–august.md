# 2025 in Rewind (May–August)

If the first part of 2025 was about asking _“what if?”_, then May to August was about asking a much harder question:

**“Okay… how do we actually build this?”**

Coming off the hackathon wins earlier in the year, there was a lot of excitement — and honestly, a bit of pressure. Winning ideas is intoxicating. But somewhere between the applause and the group photos, reality kicks in:

Ideas don’t scale themselves.

This period also happened during the long holiday after finishing my second year at university. I travelled back home from Zimbabwe to South Africa expecting a quieter few months — time to rest, reset, and spend time with family.

That is not exactly how things turned out.

---

## The Message That Started It All

Right at the end of April, something important happened: we formally formed our startup, **Proxyon Technologies**.

Around the same time, while I was preparing to travel home, I received a message saying that **AgriLease needed a working prototype by May 7th** for submission to the Presidential Innovation Awards.

The problem?

I was still travelling.

I arrived home on **May 4th**.

The next day I opened my laptop and started building.

For the next two days I worked almost non-stop. By **May 6th**, I submitted a prototype. It wasn’t a full product — just a **React Native frontend with mock data** — but it was enough to demonstrate the concept.

*[Watch the AgriLease Prototype Demo on YouTube](https://youtube.com/watch?v=YOUR_VIDEO_ID_HERE)*

Looking back now, that frantic two-day sprint was the **real beginning of AgriLease development**.

What started as a hackathon idea was now becoming actual software.

---

## Not Stopping After the Deadline

After the submission deadline passed, I could have easily paused.

Instead, I called up some of the friends I had worked with during the hackathon and said something along the lines of:

> “Let’s actually build this thing.”

Over the next three months of the holiday, a lot of real work happened.

We started thinking about:

- backend architecture
- how bookings would work
- messaging between farmers and equipment owners
- notifications
- how discovery of equipment should happen

The prototype slowly began turning into something closer to an actual product.

It was messy, experimental, and sometimes confusing — but it was progress.

---

## Life Outside the Laptop

Despite all the development work, this period wasn’t just about building software.

Being back home meant reconnecting with parts of life that university schedules sometimes push aside.

My mom introduced me to **parkrun**, and we started going together some Saturdays. I didn’t expect much from it at first, but somewhere along the way I realised something surprising:

I actually enjoyed running.

![Mom and I at Parkrun](https://placehold.co/600x400/f2f2f7/1c1c1e?text=Parkrun+with+Mom)

I also had the opportunity to **lead a few Bible studies at church**, which was a completely different kind of responsibility. Teaching and guiding discussions forced me to think deeply about things beyond code and startups.

Those moments added a kind of balance to an otherwise very technical season.

---

## A Small Upgrade That Made Development Easier

Late in **June**, I got a new phone.

It might sound like a small thing, but as someone building a mobile app, it actually mattered a lot.

Testing features like **messaging and notifications** suddenly became much easier. Instead of relying on emulators or awkward setups, I could properly test how things behaved on real devices.

Sometimes the simplest upgrades make development smoother.

---

## July: Quiet Progress

July was calmer.

There were no crazy deadlines or big external events.

Most of the month was filled with steady development work, small improvements to the app, and continuing to think about how AgriLease should evolve.

It was also my birthday month, which meant a few celebrations scattered in between the coding sessions.

Not every meaningful period of building looks dramatic. Sometimes it's just consistent progress over time.

---

## The Agricultural Show Opportunity

During the holiday, a call went out from Africa University inviting innovators to exhibit working prototypes at the **2025 Harare Agricultural Show** under the theme:

**“Building Bridges: Connecting Agriculture, Industry and Community.”**

We applied with **AgriLease**.

And we got accepted.

That meant something big: the prototype we had been building during the holiday would soon be demonstrated to real farmers, companies, and industry players.

Suddenly the stakes felt more real.

---

## August: Back to Mutare

August arrived and it was time to return to university in Mutare.

Coming back to campus meant reconnecting with friends I hadn’t seen in months, settling back into student life, and dealing with something new for me:

**finding accommodation off campus for the first time.**

It was another small step toward independence.

At the same time, preparations for the Agricultural Show were ramping up.

The app was running and demo-ready. The only major piece still missing was the **payment gateway**, but everything else was functional enough to demonstrate the concept.

---

## The Harare Agricultural Show

On **August 27th**, we travelled to Harare for the Agricultural Show.

It was my first time experiencing the event properly, and the scale of it was overwhelming. There were farmers, equipment companies, innovators, government representatives, and thousands of visitors walking through the exhibition grounds.

For a moment, I almost forgot why I was there.

But once we set up at the **Africa University booth**, the real work began.

I spent hours:

- demonstrating the app to farmers
- explaining the idea behind AgriLease
- pitching to equipment companies
- answering questions about how the system would work

I even walked around to visit some equipment company booths and pitched the idea directly to them.

![Agri Show Booth](https://placehold.co/600x400/f2f2f7/1c1c1e?text=Agri+Show+Booth)
![Pitching to Farmers](https://placehold.co/600x400/f2f2f7/1c1c1e?text=Pitching+to+Farmers)
![Agri Show Team](https://placehold.co/600x400/f2f2f7/1c1c1e?text=Agri+Show+Team)

The feedback was overwhelmingly positive.

Many farmers immediately understood the problem we were trying to solve. Some shared stories about struggling to find machinery during critical farming periods — exactly the challenge AgriLease was designed to address.

Those conversations were incredibly valuable.

For the first time, the idea was interacting with the **people it was meant for**.

---

## Foundations Over Momentum

Looking back, May to August wasn’t flashy.

There were no big awards or viral announcements.

But it might have been the most important phase of the year.

This was the period where:

- AgriLease development actually began
- Proxyon Technologies took its first steps as a startup
- the product was tested in real conversations with farmers

It was also a season of personal balance — running with my mom, teaching at church, celebrating small life moments, and learning how to manage responsibility beyond code.

It wasn’t about moving fast.

It was about moving **right**.

And by the end of August, it felt like several pieces were starting to come together.

The next chapter — **September to December** — is where things begin to converge.
